Flood Mitigation Techniques

When thinking about structural solutions for flood mitigation, it is crucial to comprehend the effectiveness of different measures in terms of the impact on flood mitigation. There may be a multitude of structural and non-structural mitigating techniques available, depending on the location and the type of flooding. Flood mitigation strategies, however, might merely minimize the effects of flooding. Heavy rain or high seas cannot be stopped, regardless matter what is done.

Structural flood mitigation

Structural measures include physical construction to lessen or eliminate the effects of potential hazards, as well as the use of engineering methods or technology to increase the resilience and hazard resistance of systems or structures. The following are typical structural methods for flood risk reduction:

  • Dams
  • Flood Levies
  • Ocean Waves Deflectors
  • Evacuation shelters

Non-Structural Flood Mitigation

Non-structural measures are actions taken to lessen the risks and effects of disasters without the use of physical construction, such as regulations and legislation, public awareness campaigns, training, and education. Common non - structural measures include:

  • Public Education Initiatives
  • Controls for Land use Planning
  • Development and Construction regulations Catchment Flood Modeling
  • Early Warning Systems
  • Create a Family Emergency Plan
  • Availability of Inforrmation and Cautions

Flood Measures

Flood Advisory

- A flood condition is indicated by a flood advisory, which is a warning. It offers recommendations for the necessary activities that the community and inhabitants of the affected region may need to take. When the hydrological situation gets worse, a warning is given. It is also issued when a condition is unquestionably getting better but still calls for vigilance.

Flood Warning

- Flood Warning is given when it is clear that there will be flooding at least 23 hours before it actually happens. This category is retained in subsequent bulletins so long as the affected areas are flooded and the associated threats exist.

Flood bulletins

- Publicity is a specific focus of flood bulletins. They are meant to inform those who live in the threatened area about the current situation as well as the future course of events. It makes recommendations on the best steps the community should take to either prevent or lessen the terrible effects of a flood.

Flood Warning Symbols

When you receive a notification of a flood alert or flood warning it's important that you know what they mean and what you need to do:

Flood Alert

What does a Flood Alert Mean?

Flooding alert is sent out by a government agency or local authority to the public regarding potential flooding.

When is an Alert issued?

It is issued 2 hours to 2 days before flooding when there is a chance that people and property are

What Causes an Alert

  • Forecasts indicate that flooding from rivers may be possible
  • Forecast of intense rainfall for rivers that respond very rapidly
  • Forecasts of high tides, surges or strong winds

What should you do?

  • Be prepared to act on your flood plan
  • Prepare an emergency flood kit of essential items
  • Avoid walking, cycling or driving through flood water
  • A Flood Alert could be upgraded to a warning so check for updates regularly

Flood Warning

What does a Flood Warning mean?

Flooding is anticipated, therefore quick action is needed.

When is a Flood Warning issued?

It is issued 30 minutes to 1 day before flooding.

What causes a Flood Warning?

  • Strong winds and high tides and surges.
  • Forecasted heavy rainfall potentially causing flash flooding of rivers
  • Forecasted flooding from rivers

Severe Flood Warning

What does a Severe Flood Warning mean?

It is life-threatening flooding and conditions.

When is a Severe Flood Warning issued?

When flooding poses a serious threat to human life or causes serious damage to communities.

What causes a Severe Flood Warning?

  • Actual flooding where the conditions pose a significant risk to life and/or widespread disruption to communities
  • Observations made in areas that were flooded.
  • A defense hole or barrier breakdown that poses a serious risk to life.

What should you do?

  • Remain in a location that is safe and has a way out.
  • Be prepared in case you have to leave your house.
  • Co-operate with emergency services.
  • If you are in immediate danger, dial 117.
