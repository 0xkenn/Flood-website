Causes of Flood

Floods can have a variety of sources. Although many different things might cause floods, most of them are caused by the elements. Floods are caused by both natural and man-made factors. Floods do severe damage to local property as well as to the flora and fauna

1. Massive Rainfall

When we consider the causes of the flood, rainfall is at the top of the list. When it rains, well-designed drainage systems and infrastructure substantially help to provide precipitation with the right path to water reservoirs, where it may fall and the water level be maintained at a steady level. However, this system is inadequate during periods of heavy precipitation, which results in flooding. Additionally, towns without such sophisticated systems suffer more as a result of their inability to deal with periods of heavy rain and flooding.

2. Overflowing of the Rivers and Storm Surge

A larger risk to their life exists for those who live near the shores of rivers and oceans. Any storm or river flood could cause a lot of problems. In order to address these problems, dams are constructed to hold back the water. However, if they are not properly maintained, they can also result in floods, which can result in significant property damage and even fatalities.

3. Collapsed Dams

As was already said, dams built to control flooding and generate energy can potentially result in major flooding. After a heavy downpour, water instantly begins to accumulate around dwellings when they are unable to contain any more. The flooding situation for the local population gets worse as these damâ€™s break

4. Deforestation

Floods are caused by deforestation, which also significantly contributes to a variety of other problems like soil erosion. The continuing destruction of trees for both personal and business reasons is referred to as deforestation. Many things have been destroyed as a result of the human-caused flood. The profusion of trees not only enhances the flora, but also significantly contributes to soil binding and crop protection. Trees are careful to block the flow of rainwater in order to prevent flooding.

5. Climate Change

Flooding is more likely to occur in a place due to climate change, which is a result of bad human conduct. Practices like deforestation raise the atmospheric concentration of carbon dioxide because plants cannot use it when they are not present. As atmospheric levels of carbon dioxide increase, more heat is trapped there, causing climate change. Climate change is a significant contributor to several natural disasters, particularly floods.

6. Greenhouse Effect

greenhouse effect refers to the process by which gases like carbon dioxide, methane, and others trap solar heat. As greenhouse gases accumulate in the atmosphere, a number of problems, including the weakening of the ozone layer, occur. This makes the emission of greenhouse gases a key factor in the occurrence of floods caused by human activities. The increase in atmospheric greenhouse gases is due to human activities such as industrial production, the burning of fossil fuels like coal and petroleum, and other similar activities.

Types of Flooding

Flooding can be classified into several types. Although there are many categories into which the many flood kinds can be grouped, this is simply a summary. Floods typically appear in a variety of forms

1.Surge Floods

These floods are the ones caused by tidal surges, storm tides, storm surges, and other occurrences that take place in the ocean or sea. They are a particular type of coastal flood that occur along the shore. When dry land areas of the world are flooded in seawater for a variety of reasons, floods along the coast result. Depending on how much seawater or ocean is involved in the storm surges and hurricanes, floods can spread more or less catastrophically

2. Pluvial Flood

Contrary to popular belief, both urban regions and rural villages are susceptible to pluvial floods. This defies the notion that flooding would only cause damage to those who were near a body of water. It takes place as a result of extended periods of intense rain and any water body overflowing. Small floods cause a lot of harm even if they may not contain a lot of water. Both the ecology and the infrastructure have been severely damaged by this disaster. The two primary forms of pluvial floods are as follows:

i: Surface Water Floods
These floods occur when the urban drainage system malfunctions, allowing water to overflow into the streets and residences of the neighborhood and disturb daily life. Despite the fact that it doesn't represent a serious threat to life and develops gradually, giving the local population time to evacuate to safer locations, it nonetheless causes substantial resource devastation.

ii: Flash Floods
They are the kind of floods when water spreads swiftly and at a high speed as a result of a brief but intense downpour. These floods may also come from the abrupt discharge of a dam or water that has been stored higher up. These floods occur very swiftly and spread across the land in a very short period of time. Additionally, when it comes to destruction, these floods are especially damaging due to the hurtling debris that is frequently swept up in the water flow and the extremely fast water flow that occurs during flash floods. Greater environmental harm is caused by it than by surface water floods

3. Floods caused by the Spilling of Reservoirs

Reservoir spills could result in floods downstream. The overflowing could have occurred naturally or as a result of the rese rvoir manager opening the gates. The reservoir owner, such as the Irrigation Department, will offer notice in any scenario

4. River floods

This is characterized by slow riverbank overflows brought on by heavy rainfall that lasts for a long time. The size of the river and the amount of rainfall affect the places that flood during river events. Although river floods rarely result in fatalities, they can nonetheless cause significant economic harm

5. Urban floods

This flood happens when a city's or town's drainage system is unable to adequately absorb the water following a si gnificant downpour. In an urban setting, poor natural drainage can also lead to flooding. Driving is quite unsafe due to water spilling into the road. Even though the water may only be a few inches deep, urban floods can seriously harm structures.
